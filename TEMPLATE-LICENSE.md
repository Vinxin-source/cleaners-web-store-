# Template Usage License (Draft — Not Legal Advice)

This is a plain-language starting point, not a lawyer-drafted contract. If you're selling this at real volume, it's worth having a lawyer review or adapt something like this for your country/state. Two common tiers used by template marketplaces (like ThemeForest/Creative Market) are outlined below — pick one model or adapt it.

---

## Option A — Simple single-use license (easiest to manage)
Each sale = one rebrand, for one end business, deployed once.

**The buyer (designer or business) may:**
- Rebrand this template with their own or their client's business details
- Deploy it to one live website per purchase
- Modify the code freely for that one deployment

**The buyer may NOT:**
- Resell, sublicense, or redistribute the original template files themselves
- Use one purchase to deploy the same template for multiple different end clients (each end client = a separate purchase)
- Claim they built the template from scratch if reselling design services around it (they can say they customized/deployed it — just not that they authored the base template)

---

## Option B — Designer/reseller license (for other designers buying it to resell)
If you sell this to *other designers* who intend to rebrand it repeatedly for their own clients:

- One purchase = the right to rebrand and deploy this template for **unlimited end clients**, as long as the designer is doing the reselling (not redistributing the raw template files for others to resell again)
- Typically priced higher than Option A (since it's a business-in-a-box for them, not a single site)
- Consider requiring designers to remove any of your branding/attribution before their own resale, or requiring a small attribution line if you want ongoing brand exposure

---

## Suggested pricing structure (based on current market norms for template resale)
- Single-use (Option A): **$50–$150** per license if selling the template alone (no setup service)
- Designer/reseller (Option B): **$300–$800** one-time, or a smaller recurring fee per active deployment

## What to include when you sell it
- These files + README
- A short Loom/screen-recording walkthrough of editing `config.js` (this alone reduces support requests dramatically)
- Optional: a support window (e.g. "30 days of setup help included") — be explicit about what's included vs. what costs extra

## Basic legal hygiene (do this before selling at any volume)
- State clearly: sold "as-is," no warranty of fitness for a particular purpose
- State who owns the code after purchase (you retain the base template copyright; they own their rebranded deployment)
- Keep a simple record of who you sold licenses to and under which tier — useful if a dispute ever comes up
